The Echoes of a Scream:

The Existential Aye-Aye

A sketch of a stressed and exhausted aye-aye working at a computer screams at their "human" condition. This sketch was created by myself and was inspired by Edvard Munch's The Scream, one of my personal favorite works of art.

Edvard Munch

The Scream was a part of Munch's semi-autobiographical cycle “The Frieze of Life.” During the later years of his life he created four iterations of The Scream. The first painting was created using oil, tempera, and pastel on cardboard in 1893, it currently resides in the National Gallery of Art, Oslo. The next two were both done in pastel the first in 1893, which is located at the Munch Museum, Oslo, the second was created in 1895, and is part of a private collection) The final version was a tempera painting created in 1910, which is now a part of the National Gallery of Art, Oslo. There is also a lithographic version that was created in 1895 as well.

The four different iterations of "The Scream," by Edvard Munch, 1893,1893,1895, and 1910
